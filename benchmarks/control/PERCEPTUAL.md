> proxy on the synthetic data вЂ” a real step beyond eps-MSE (it samples + measures the distribution)

## вљ пёЏ The honest headline: the proxy does NOT crown a sample-quality winner
The robust distributional metric (**MMD**, Gaussian kernel, bandwidth fixed from the real set) puts
**16 of 17 optimizers in a tight band (0.36вЂ“0.45)** вЂ” differences within noise (1 seed, 192 stochastic
samples). Only **Adai (0.81)** is a clear outlier, and that *is* visually confirmed (its grid is
near-flat, almost no structure). Visually, every other optimizer produces **soft/noisy approximations**
of the real diagonal-sinusoid stripes вЂ” none crisp, none clearly better. **On this synthetic proxy,
sample-level quality can't separate the field.** That itself is the finding: the eps-MSE differences
we measured do not translate into a clean perceptual winner here вЂ” the real verdict needs SDXL/Flux FID.

> Note on the metric: the first cut used the standard cubic-polynomial KID, which **exploded (10вЃ·)** on
> a handful of optimizers вЂ” not because their samples were garbage (the grids looked normal) but because
> the cubic kernel amplifies a *scale/contrast* offset. Switched to a bounded Gaussian-MMD with a
> real-data-fixed bandwidth (floor real-vs-real в‰€ 0). Lesson logged; the old KID is not trustworthy here.

## Verdict
- **Adai** is the only clear sample-quality failure (confirmed numerically + visually) вЂ” consistent with
  its battery weakness. Drop.
- **For everyone else, the proxy can't decide** вЂ” and that is the honest result. The interesting,
  trustworthy signal is the **two-failure-mode dichotomy** (under-contrast+near-train vs
  correct-scale+unstable-sampling), which would be the thing to probe on a real model.
- **The real judge remains SDXL/Flux LoRA + FID/KID + CLIP + a human A/B** вЂ” this proxy is a cheap
  directional check, not that. It rules out Adai and flags what to watch (contrast & sampling stability),
  but a perceptual winner among the rest needs the real test.
